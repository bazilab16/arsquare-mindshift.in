Hey There!

Welcome to our online patient registration portal, "One4Patient"! We value your privacy and are committed to ensuring that your information is secure. As part of our compliance with HIPAA regulations, we have implemented a secure and convenient way for you to access and manage your medical records.

To complete your registration process, we will redirect you to our HIPAA-compliant portal, "One4Patient." Once registered, you can access your medical records, test results, and treatment plans. In addition, you can conveniently communicate with your healthcare professionals through this secure platform.

To ensure the confidentiality of your information, we will share your login credentials via email and text message to the mobile number provided during registration. We understand the importance of safeguarding your data and have taken necessary measures to protect it.

If you have any concerns regarding the security of email communication, please let us know, and we will make alternative arrangements to suit your preferences. Your peace of mind is our priority.

Thank you for choosing our online patient registration portal. We look forward to providing you with exceptional care and a seamless experience.

Case Studies

  • Case Study 1: Sarah :

    Sarah is a 16-year-old high school student who has been struggling with anxiety and panic attacks. She finds it challenging to attend classes and participate in group activities due to her fear of being judged by others. Through Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Sarah learns to identify and challenge her negative thought patterns. She also practices relaxation techniques and gradually exposes herself to anxiety-provoking situations. Over time, Sarah's anxiety decreases, and she becomes more confident and engaged in her school community.

  • Case Study 2: James :

    James, a 17-year-old student, has been experiencing symptoms of depression. He frequently isolates himself from his peers, exhibits low motivation, and struggles with concentration. James begins Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to address his negative thinking patterns. With the support of his therapist, James learns to identify and challenge his distorted thoughts, set realistic goals, and engage in pleasurable activities. As a result, James experiences a reduction in depressive symptoms and is able to focus better on his schoolwork.

  • Case Study 3: Emily :

    Emily, an 18-year-old college student, has a history of self-harm and struggles with emotional dysregulation. She often feels overwhelmed by her intense emotions and has difficulty maintaining stable relationships. Dialectical-Behavioral Therapy (DBT) is recommended for Emily. Through individual therapy, group skills training, and phone coaching, Emily learns mindfulness techniques, distress tolerance skills, emotion regulation strategies, and interpersonal effectiveness skills. As Emily practices these skills, she becomes more resilient, gains control over her emotions, and develops healthier relationships with her peers.

  • Case Study 4: David :

    David, a 15-year-old student, has been diagnosed with Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). He finds it challenging to stay focused in class, complete assignments, and manage his time effectively. David starts Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to address his difficulties. With the help of his therapist, David develops strategies to improve his organizational skills, set priorities, and break down tasks into manageable steps. As a result, David experiences an improvement in his academic performance and gains confidence in his abilities.

  • Case Study 5: Rachel :

    Rachel, a 14-year-old student, has been experiencing symptoms of social anxiety. She avoids social situations, including speaking in class and participating in extracurricular activities. Rachel begins Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to overcome her fears. Through exposure therapy and cognitive restructuring, Rachel gradually confronts her anxiety-provoking situations and challenges her negative beliefs about social interactions. Over time, Rachel's social anxiety decreases, and she is able to engage more confidently with her peers and teachers.

  • Case Study 6: Michael :

    Michael, a 19-year-old university student, has been struggling with substance abuse issues. His substance use has negatively impacted his academic performance and relationships. Michael enters a comprehensive treatment program that incorporates Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Dialectical-Behavioral Therapy (DBT). Through individual therapy, group counseling, and skills training, Michael learns to manage cravings, develop healthier coping mechanisms, improve his self-esteem, and build a support network. With time, Michael achieves sobriety, improves his academic standing, and rebuilds his relationships.

  • Case Study 7: Olivia :

    Olivia, a 16-year-old high school student, has been experiencing symptoms of an eating disorder. She struggles with body image issues, restrictive eating patterns, and excessive exercise. Olivia begins Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to address her disordered eating behaviors and distorted thoughts. With the support of her therapist, Olivia challenges her negative body image beliefs, develops a healthier relationship with food, and learns coping skills to manage her emotions. Over time, Olivia's eating disorder symptoms decrease, and she gains a more positive sense of self.

  • Case Study 8: Alex :

    Alex, a 17-year-old student, has been dealing with anger management issues. He frequently gets into physical altercations with his peers and has difficulty controlling his temper. Alex starts Dialectical-Behavioral Therapy (DBT) to address his emotional dysregulation. Through individual therapy and skills training, Alex learns mindfulness techniques, distress tolerance skills, and emotion regulation strategies. As a result, Alex becomes more aware of his emotions, develops healthier ways to manage anger, and improves his interpersonal relationships.

  • Case Study 9: Lily :

    Lily, a 15-year-old student, has been struggling with self-esteem issues and perfectionism. She constantly feels pressure to excel academically and socially, which leads to excessive stress and anxiety. Lily begins Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to address her negative self-perceptions and high standards. With the help of her therapist, Lily learns to challenge her perfectionistic thinking, set realistic goals, practice self-compassion, and engage in self-care activities. Over time, Lily's self-esteem improves, and she experiences a reduction in anxiety symptoms.

  • Case Study 10: Ethan :

    Ethan, a 16-year-old student, has been diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). He struggles with social interactions, communication, and sensory sensitivities. Ethan participates in a therapy program that combines Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and specialized interventions for individuals with ASD. Through CBT techniques adapted for ASD, Ethan learns social skills, emotion regulation strategies, and coping mechanisms for sensory challenges. As a result, Ethan's social and emotional functioning improves, and he is better equipped to navigate his school environment
