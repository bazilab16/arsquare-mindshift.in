Featured

Mental health problems are common among students in India. According to a study by the National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHANS), nearly 13% of adolescents in India experience some form of mental health problem. Mental health problems can have a significant impact on students' academic performance and overall well-being. Students with mental health problems are more likely to experience academic difficulties, such as poor grades and absenteeism. They are also more likely to experience social and emotional problems, such as anxiety, depression, and loneliness. Mental health problems can lead to serious long-term consequences, such as substance abuse, suicide, and chronic health conditions. Students who experience mental health problems are more likely to engage in risky behaviors, such as substance abuse and unprotected sex. They are also more likely to attempt suicide or die by suicide. Additionally, mental health problems can lead to chronic health conditions, such as heart disease, stroke, and diabetes. Mental health problems can also have a negative impact on the Indian economy. According to a study by the World Health Organization (WHO), mental health problems cost the Indian economy an estimated $1.5 trillion per year in lost productivity and healthcare costs. Despite the importance of student mental health, there is a significant stigma associated with mental health problems in India. This stigma can prevent students from seeking help for mental health problems. Additionally, there is a shortage of mental health professionals in India, which makes it difficult for students to access the care they need. It is important to promote student mental health in India by: Raising awareness of mental health problems and reducing stigma. This can be done through educational campaigns and by talking openly about mental health. Making mental health services more accessible and affordable. This can be done by increasing the number of mental health professionals in India and by making mental health services more affordable for students. Creating a supportive school environment. This can be done by training teachers to identify and support students with mental health problems and by creating a school climate where students feel comfortable talking about their mental health. By taking these steps, we can create a more supportive and inclusive environment for all students in India.

Mission:

Our mission is to prioritize student mental health in India by raising awareness, reducing stigma, and providing accessible and affordable mental health services. We aim to create a supportive school environment where students feel comfortable talking about their mental health and receive the necessary support.

Vision:

We envision a future where student mental health is valued and prioritized in India. We strive for a society where all students have access to the mental health resources they need, leading to improved academic performance, overall well-being, and reduced stigma surrounding mental health.

  • Empathy:

    We believe in understanding and empathizing with the unique mental health challenges faced by students in India. We strive to provide compassionate care and support to promote their well-being.

  • Collaboration:

    We value collaboration among students, parents, teachers, and mental health professionals to create a comprehensive support system for student mental health.

  • Accessibility:

    We are committed to making mental health services accessible and affordable for all students in India, regardless of their background or financial capabilities.

  • Education:

    We emphasize the importance of education and awareness about mental health issues to reduce stigma and promote a culture of understanding and empathy.

  • Inclusivity:

    We recognize the importance of inclusivity and aim to create a safe and supportive environment where students from all backgrounds can thrive mentally, emotionally, and academically.
